Claims Makers

Organizations In Support of Access to Higher Education for Undocumented Immigrants

The Coalition for Tuition Equality (CTE) at the University of Michigan

CTE is a student-led advocacy group that focuses on promoting tuition equality for all students, regardless of immigration status. They have been active in advocating for policies within the University of Michigan that would allow undocumented students to qualify for in-state tuition rates, making higher education more affordable.

United We Dream (UWD): The largest immigrant youth-led community in the country.

United We Dream advocates for the dignity and fair treatment of undocumented immigrants, including the right to education. United We Dream has been actively campaigning for policies that support undocumented students in higher education, such as state-level versions of the DREAM Act.

Organizations Against Access to Higher Education for Undocumented Immigrants

Federation for American Immigration Reform (FAIR)

FAIR argues that providing access to higher education for undocumented immigrants incentivizes illegal immigration and is unfair to legal residents and citizens.

Center for Immigration Studies (CIS)

CIS often publishes research suggesting that the admission of undocumented immigrants to institutions like the University of Michigan places a burden on taxpayers and can lead to a reduction in resources for citizens and legal residents
